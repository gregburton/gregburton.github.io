Greg Burton

Web developer. Durham, NC.



It all began about 15 years ago, with a middle-school foray into HTML. The initial results of those efforts have been lost to history. A hobby quickly grew into building and maintaining websites pro-bono for the church and library in my small Vermont hometown, which continued into my college years.

Skip ahead a bit, and I decided to revisit these web development roots. I relocated from Boston, MA to Durham, NC, and enrolled in an intensive 3-month Rails Engineering course at The Iron Yard Academy. I spent 80-100 hours a week learning the ins-and-outs of Ruby on Rails and associated technologies.

In the time between those first two paragraphs, I amassed eight years of higher-ed IT support experience at three different institutions, obtained a number of technical certifications, and earned a master's degree in History. This multi-faceted background has been an asset in several realms, whether it be critical thinking and analysis, producing quality work on a quick turnaround, or communicating with clients and colleagues effectively.

When I'm not coding, you'll probably find me outside running, going to the shows of bands that most people haven't heard of, playing pick-up softball, rummaging through the vinyl bins at the record store, managing my fantasy baseball rosters, noodling on the guitar, getting coffee, or cracking wise.

I am currently looking for a junior developer position in the Durham, NC area. Poke around, take a look at some of my work, and if you like what you see, please drop me a line.

ramblethru

This was my final project for The Iron Yard. I worked on it with Noah Holmes and Danielle Heckman over the course of three weeks. It is a travel planning website that aggregates tips from several APIs and other ramblethru users, while presenting the data in an intuitive and elegant interface. Tips can be saved for easy access later, such as from your smartphone when you are out at your destination.

Last Week

This was another weekend assignment that I initally worked on with Noah Holmes and Rebecca Conley. This was an early foray into working with an API (in this case, for last.fm, as well as AJAX and jQuery. It returns a list of your friends and the top artist for each from the past week, along with play count. I came up with the idea for this project because this was a feature that was notably missing from the last.fm website.
