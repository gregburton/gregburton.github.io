Greg Burton

Web developer. Durham, NC.



It all began about 15 years ago, with a middle-school foray into HTML. A hobby quickly grew into building and maintaining websites pro-bono for the church and library in my small Vermont hometown. Skip ahead a bit, and I decided to revisit these web development roots. After a few years in Boston, MA, I moved to Durham, NC and enrolled in an intensive three-month Rails Engineering course at The Iron Yard Academy. There, I spent 80-100 hours a week learning the ins-and-outs of Ruby on Rails and associated technologies.

In addition, I have eight years of higher-ed IT support experience and a master's degree in history. When I'm not coding, you'll probably find me outside running, going to shows, managing my fantasy baseball rosters, getting coffee, or cracking wise.

ramblethru

This was my final project for The Iron Yard. I worked on it with Noah Holmes and Danielle Heckman over the course of three weeks. It is a travel planning website that aggregates tips from several APIs and other ramblethru users, while presenting the data in an intuitive and elegant interface. Tips can be saved for easy access later, such as from your smartphone when you are out at your destination.

Last Week

This was a weekend assignment that I initially worked on with Noah Holmes and Rebecca Conley. It was an early foray into working with an API (in this case, for last.fm), as well as AJAX and jQuery. The request returns a list of your friends and the top artist for each from the past week, along with play count and links. I came up with the idea for this project because this was a feature that was notably missing from the last.fm website.
