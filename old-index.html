Greg Burton

Web developer. Durham, NC.





I recently relocated to Durham, NC after a quarter-century in New England. I recently graduated from the intensive 12-week Rails Engineering course at The Iron Yard Academy in December of 2014, and am currently looking for a junior developer position in the Triangle.

Previously, I amassed eight years of higher ed IT support experience at three different institutions, obtained a number of technical certifications, and earned a master's degree in American history.

Here is a portfolio of work that I've done.

ramblethru

This was my final project for The Iron Yard. I worked on it with Noah Holmes[link] and Danielle Heckman[link] over the course of three weeks. It is is a crowdsourced travel planning website, that aims to help cut down on browser tabs and search times while still helping you find the best things to do at your destination.

Check out the project at ramblethru.com

Video of ramblethru presentation at Demo Day

Source code on GitHub

Mo' Problems

This was a weekend work assignment that I collaborated on with Nick Passarella[link]. He had to be out of town for the weekend, so we dilligently planned out the structure and division of labor for the site in the couple of hours we had before he left. It was excellent practice in collaborating on a project remotely.

Check out the project on Heroku

Source code on GitHub

Last Week

This was another weekend work assignment that I collaborated on with Noah Holmes[link] and Rebecca Conley[link]. You enter in your last.fm username, and it returns a list of 10 of your friends and who their top artist for the week was. This was our first foray into working with APIs, and as a music lover, I came up with the idea for this because this was not a feature that the last.fm site proper actually had.

Check out the project on Heroku[not actually on Heroku yet]
